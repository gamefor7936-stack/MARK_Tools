Turn Your Smartphone into a Professional AI Scanner

Gone are the days of relying on bulky hardware scanners to digitize receipts, identity cards, or signed legal contracts. Taking a photo with your smartphone is fast, but it often results in terrible angles, dark shadows, and unreadable text. The MARK_Tools Document Scanner utilizes advanced Computer Vision to magically flatten, crop, and enhance your photos, converting them into crisp, professional A4 PDFs.

Most mobile scanner apps are infamous for locking basic features behind expensive subscriptions, watermarking your documents, or secretly harvesting your sensitive data. By running OpenCV (Open Source Computer Vision Library) compiled into WebAssembly directly inside your browser, we guarantee 100% data privacy.

Smart Perspective Deskew

Took a photo of a document sitting on a table from an awkward, slanted angle? No problem. By dragging the four blue corner handles to match the edges of your paper, our engine uses a mathematical matrix called Perspective Transformation to warp and flatten the image, making it appear as if it was scanned perfectly from above.

Adaptive B&W Thresholding

Lighting conditions are rarely perfect. The "B&W Document" filter applies an Adaptive Gaussian Threshold to your image. This algorithm analyzes small clusters of pixels to eliminate background shadows, turning the paper pure white and the text solid black. You can use the "Strength" slider to fine-tune the noise reduction.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I combine multiple scans into one PDF?

Absolutely! Just click "Add Page" on the sidebar to upload another photo. The system will hold them in a queue. Once you have deskewed and applied filters to all your pages, clicking "Save PDF" will compile them all into a single, multi-page document.

Why does the browser freeze for a second when applying a filter?

Running complex graphical matrix calculations (like grayscale conversion, blurring, and perspective warping) on high-resolution, multi-megapixel smartphone photos requires massive CPU power. Because we prioritize your privacy by doing this completely offline, your browser is doing all the heavy mathematical lifting locally.
